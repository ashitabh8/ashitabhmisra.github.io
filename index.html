Update: Our research on Latency-Constrained Input-Aware Quantization of Time Series Inference Workflows at the Edge has been accepted at IEEE International Conference on Computer Communications (INFOCOM). As an A* conference with a 19% acceptance rate this year, we're honored to be presenting this work in London, UK.

I am Ashitabh Misra, a 4th-year PhD student at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign in the Department of Computer Science.

I am fortunate to be advised by Dr. Tarek Abdelzaher.

My research is focused on on-device, low-latency machine learning technologies. I specialize in developing adaptive compression techniques for neural networks and probabilistic models, with the goal of making AI more efficient and accessible on resource-constrained devices. Specifically, I am exploring how to leverage physics-based insights and time-series characteristics to estimate optimal compression implementations that achieve maximum accuracy while incurring minimal latency and memory overhead.
